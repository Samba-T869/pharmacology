Simple summary

1. Selective toxicity & Drug resistance in parasites

Selective toxicity

Definition Mechanism Example
Ability of antimicrobial to inhibit/kill pathogen with minimal host damage
  • Selective inhibition of cell wall synthesis
  • Inhibition of unique metabolic pathways
  • Selective blockade of microbial ribosomes
  • Disruption of parasite tubulin polymerization
  • Penicillins, vancomycin
  • Sulfonamides, trimethoprim
  • Macrolides, tetracyclines
  • Benzimidazoles (albendazole)

Drug resistance

Drug Mechanism of resistance Dose (adult)
Chloroquine PfCRT mutation → reduced drug accumulation in digestive vacuole 600 mg base, then 300 mg at 6/24/48h
Metronidazole Reduced nitroreductase activity; efflux pumps 750 mg tid ×10d (amoebiasis)
Sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine Mutated DHFR/DHPS enzymes 3 tablets single dose (IPTp)

2. Disinfectants and antiseptic

First line drugs / common agents

Drug Mechanism of action Typical use/concentration Adverse effects
Chlorhexidine Disrupts cell membrane, precipitates cytoplasm 0.05–4% (surgical scrub, oral rinse)
  • Ototoxicity (if in ear)
  • Staining of teeth
Povidone-iodine Oxidation/halogenation of proteins 7.5–10% solution (preoperative)
  • Contact dermatitis
  • Thyroid suppression (neonates)
Ethanol (70%) Protein denaturation, lipid dissolution hand sanitizer, skin prep
  • Drying, irritation
  • Flammable

Second line / specific agents

Drug Mechanism of action Use/dose Adverse effects
Hydrogen peroxide Oxidative burst via free radicals 3% (wound cleansing)
  • Tissue toxicity
  • Embolism if under pressure
Sodium hypochlorite Oxidizes sulfhydryl enzymes 0.05–0.5% (Dakin solution)
  • Irritant
  • Corrosive
